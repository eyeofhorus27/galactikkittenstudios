  • The Restaurant at the Edge of the Galaxy

    Interior Design Creative Direction

  • Role
    Interior Designer

A small homage to one of my favorite books, "The restaurant at the end of the universe," this concept was conceived from my obsession of looking at 3D geometric art for endless hours on Dribble and the constant looping of Sevdaliza music videos playing in the background. Looking at the arrangement of these 3D object, I thought to myself, what if I was shrank and then transported into this digital world and interacted with these objects? What would I do? Would I slide down that cone? Would that even be possible? Maybe hang from this weird golden hook? Or would I eat at the top of this assymetrical ladder?

This in turn sprang up the idea of an interactive restaurant, something like "The Museum of Ice Cream" meets food but without all the sterile plasticy creepiness. Then I thought of where this restaurant would reside and what kind of guests it would attract. It ended up being at the edge of the galaxy frequented by my favorite Lyran Femme Bots. And of course, I wanted everything including the food and utensils to be geometric. Finally, molecular gastronomy could not be absent in this world since animals and plants have gone extinct and olives are now grown in labs having a liquid gel form.
