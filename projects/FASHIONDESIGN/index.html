This fashion clothing collection features outfits all 3D-printed from the highest quality flexible filaments and printers. Each piece of clothing is 3D printed in pieces and assembled together much like IKEA furniture. The outfits themselves are inspired from science fiction, Japanese traditional attire and retro-futuristic styles like "Blade Runner" & "Metropolis." Based on the concept, these Femme Bots reside on Vega, the brightest star on the constellation of Lyra. Each piece is made for a specific character that exists and functions within the universe. Some examples are the Galactic Queen, who rules over the whole galaxy, the Lyran Army that protects and fights for Lyra and the Jester that is a god-like AI weapon with unparalleled powers.

This concept is simply an idea on how these 3D printed clothes can be utilized to represent society in a Sci-fi feature or show or for a performance. I want the outfits to be affordable enough to add high production value to low-budget projects and indie artists but also allow everyday people to inspire others at festivals. I also want to be part of the movement towards customized clothes that people can print from the comfort of their own homes.

The patterns chosen are usually geometric featuring cubes and a variety of fractal-like patterns. This of course is on one hand practical for the 3D printer to replicate and on the other hand, tries to emulate a sort of constructed nature. The ultimate goal is to make biodegradable clothes that are an extension of nature. The patterns also provide that futuristic “feel” and create excitement. The reason for the Japanese inspiration is because Japan (mainly Harajuku & Shibuya) is usually the frontrunner for edgy futuristic outfits that often draw from traditional elements. Plus everything looks cool when it looks Japanese.

The women wearing these clothes are powerful entities and these outfits make sure to convey that vibe. They are literally out of this world and the point is to empower and inspire. The colors chosen are deeps reds, electric blues, rose pinks and golds to once again show that the women wearing them are bold and regal.

I'm hoping one day these ome prototypes can evolve to have more functional purposes aka become wearable technology. For example, they will be able to record your pulse, how many steps you took, make phone calls and allow you to merge AR with the real world.
